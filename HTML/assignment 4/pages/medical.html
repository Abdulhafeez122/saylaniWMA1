Calpol is a popular medicine used to relieve pain and fever, especially in children. Its main active ingredient is paracetamol (acetaminophen). Calpol is commonly used to treat conditions like colds, flu, teething pain, sore throat, and headaches. It is available in different forms such as syrup for children and tablets for adults. It is important to follow the dosage instructions carefully to avoid overdose, especially in young children. Calpol is widely trusted by parents and doctors due to its gentle and effective action.

Pyodine is an antiseptic solution used to prevent and treat infections in wounds, cuts, and burns. It contains Povidone-Iodine as the active ingredient, which is effective against a wide range of bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Pyodine is commonly used in hospitals and homes for cleaning wounds and preparing the skin before surgery. It helps in faster healing by keeping the affected area free from germs. Pyodine is available in different forms such as solution, ointment, and surgical scrub.

Titan injection is commonly used as a Tetanus Toxoid (TT) vaccine. It helps protect the body from tetanus, a serious bacterial infection caused by Clostridium tetani. This bacteria enters the body through wounds, cuts, or injuries and can cause painful muscle stiffness and spasms, especially in the neck and jaw (also called "lockjaw").
