Skardu

Skardu, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan, is a stunning valley surrounded by majestic mountains and serene lakes. It is not only famous for its natural beauty but also for its rich history and vibrant culture. Historical landmarks such as the Skardu Fort (also known as Kharpocho Fort) and Shigar Fort tell stories of ancient rulers and Tibetan-inspired architecture. The local Balti culture is deeply rooted in Tibetan and Islamic traditions, and the people of Skardu are known for their hospitality, simplicity, and warm nature. Traditional music, handicrafts, and festivals like Nowruz are an important part of their lifestyle. The region is also a gateway to some of the world's highest peaks, including K2, attracting adventurers and historians alike. A visit to Skardu offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural wonder that leaves every traveler amazed.
